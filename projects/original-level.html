01 · Overview

Designing a route the player gradually learns to own.

The level is structured around a legible critical path with layered side spaces, vertical crossings, and a loopback that changes how the player understands the environment on return.

This first framework will be refined with final map metrics, encounter beats, and development-time details after the walkthrough is uploaded.

02 · Design pillars

Three questions guided the blockout.

01

Can the player read the goal?

Use sightlines, contrast, framing, and landmark relationships to establish direction without removing discovery.

02

Does movement reveal new meaning?

Layer routes vertically so familiar spaces gain new context when viewed from above, below, or across a gap.

03

Does the return feel earned?

Use the elevator and lever system as a functional shortcut that compresses traversal and confirms spatial understanding.

03 · Level flow

Critical path, optional routes, and layered loopbacks.

Whole-level flow

One critical route, multiple changes of pace.

The macro layout connects exterior approaches, interior spaces, a cave route, and the church arena. Optional branches add pickups and combat pressure before reconnecting with the main route.

Church combat sandbox

Routes overlap across four levels.

The church is treated as a compact multi-level sandbox rather than a single corridor. Ground-floor movement, elevated paths, drop-downs, and shortcuts repeatedly cross the same combat space from new angles.

04 · Design breakdown

Iteration made visible.

01

Visual guidance

Landmarks, elevation, framed openings, and contrasting interaction surfaces keep the next destination readable without reducing the space to a corridor.

1 / 2
02

Layered space and loopback

The church turns vertical traversal into a change of perspective, while the elevator converts that spatial knowledge into a useful return route.

1 / 2
03

Scale and camera iteration

Playtesting exposed places where terrain and narrow circulation interfered with the third-person camera. Clearances were adjusted to keep movement and combat readable.

1 / 2
04

Lighting pass

Window light alone left critical circulation too dark. Selective fill lighting improves path readability while preserving contrast and atmosphere.
