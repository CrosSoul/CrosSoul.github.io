An Analysis of Elden Ring’s Mainstream Success

A data-informed study of how open-world structure turns fixed difficulty gates into player-directed preparation, exploration, and return.

Role
Research Lead
Format
Academic Group Study
Tools
RStudio · Public Data
Focus
Player Agency & Difficulty

01 · Research question

Why did an uncompromisingly difficult game reach a mainstream audience?

The study begins with an apparent market contradiction: difficult action RPGs generally reach smaller audiences, yet Elden Ring became both one of the hardest and one of the best-selling games in its comparison set.

Its central argument is structural. Elden Ring does not make individual encounters easy. Instead, the open world lets players postpone a fixed obstacle, choose another route, gather resources, improve their build, and return on their own terms.

Difficulty remains intact, but failure is less likely to become a complete progression dead end.

02 · Market pattern

A visible outlier in difficulty and sales.

Interpretation

Difficulty alone does not explain the result.

Other demanding FromSoftware titles achieved strong but substantially smaller sales. Elden Ring’s position suggests that the surrounding progression structure changed how a broader audience experienced the same underlying design philosophy.

The chart establishes the anomaly; it does not by itself prove that open-world structure caused the sales outcome.

04 · Contribution & limits

Research-led design analysis, presented with appropriate limits.

I led the research design, built the datasets, conducted the comparisons, created the RStudio visualizations, and developed the central argument and overall structure. Other group members contributed portions of the writing within the established outline.

The study combines public sales reports and estimates, community difficulty ratings, and PlayStation trophy data. These sources are useful for exploratory analysis, but each carries limitations in completeness, subjectivity, or cross-game comparability.

The observed patterns support a design interpretation: player-controlled routing can make high difficulty more approachable without removing the challenge itself. They are not presented as proof that one design decision alone caused Elden Ring’s commercial success.
